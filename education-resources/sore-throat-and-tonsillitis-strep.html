Sore throat and tonsillitis (including strep throat)

Patient & family education. Updated January 2026. This information is for education only and does not replace individual medical advice.

Overview

Most sore throats are viral. Group A strep is more common in school-aged children and needs antibiotics to prevent complications.

When to test for strep

  • Fever, sore throat, and tender neck nodes without cough.
  • White patches on tonsils can occur in both viral and strep infections.

Home care

  • Fluids, warm drinks, and soft foods.
  • Pain control if needed.

Seek urgent care if

  • Breathing difficulty, drooling, or inability to swallow.
  • Dehydration or very severe pain.

Cough, wheeze, and bronchiolitis

Patient & family education. Updated January 2026. This information is for education only and does not replace individual medical advice.

Overview

Bronchiolitis is a viral lower airway infection common in infants. Wheeze can also occur with asthma in older children.

Home care

  • Keep the nose clear (saline and suction in infants).
  • Offer small frequent feeds.
  • Monitor breathing and hydration.

Seek urgent care now if

  • Fast breathing, chest retractions, grunting, or bluish lips.
  • Apnea (pauses in breathing), poor feeding, or lethargy.

Healthy sleep habits for children

Patient & family education. Updated January 2026. This information is for education only and does not replace individual medical advice.

Why sleep matters

Sleep affects immunity, behavior, school performance, appetite regulation, and gut function.

Tips

  • Consistent bedtime and wake time.
  • Screen-free wind-down routine (1–2 hours before bed).
  • Bedroom: dark, cool, and quiet.

When to discuss with your clinician

  • Loud snoring, pauses in breathing, or severe daytime sleepiness.
  • Persistent insomnia or frequent night waking.

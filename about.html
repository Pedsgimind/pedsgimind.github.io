About Dr. Mohammad Hussein

Dr. Mohammad Hussein is a Royal College–certified pediatrician with fellowship training in pediatric gastroenterology, hepatology and nutrition. He cares for infants, children and adolescents with digestive, liver, feeding and nutrition problems in Canada, the Gulf region and Iraq, with a calm, family-centred and trauma-informed approach.

Training & Certification

  • MBChB – Baghdad College of Medicine, University of Baghdad, Iraq
  • Pediatrics Residency – Children’s Hospital, Western University, London, Ontario, Canada
  • Fellowship – Pediatric Gastroenterology, Hepatology & Nutrition, McMaster Children’s Hospital, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada
  • FRCPC – Pediatrics, Royal College of Physicians & Surgeons of Canada
  • FAAP – Fellow of the American Academy of Pediatrics
  • Licentiate of the Medical Council of Canada (LMCC)
  • Additional training in liver biopsy, capsule endoscopy, pH-impedance testing, hydrogen/methane breath testing and anorectal manometry

Clinical Focus & Interests

  • Inflammatory bowel disease (ulcerative colitis, Crohn disease, very early-onset IBD)
  • Celiac disease, intestinal lymphangiectasia and other malabsorption disorders
  • Functional and motility problems: chronic abdominal pain, constipation, fecal incontinence, reflux and vomiting syndromes
  • Feeding difficulties, selective and restrictive eating, obesity and metabolic risk
  • Gastrointestinal and nutritional issues in children with neurodevelopmental conditions, including Autism Spectrum Disorder
  • Endoscopic care for children: diagnostic and therapeutic upper endoscopy and colonoscopy, management of GI bleeding and foreign body ingestion

Practice Background

  • Current practice at Al Farabi Gastroenterology & Endoscopy Center, Diyala Health Ministry, Iraq
  • Previous consultant pediatric GI practice at Maternity & Children Hospital, Al Qassim Health Cluster, Saudi Arabia
  • Eight years of community pediatric and pediatric GI practice in Ontario (Oakville and Milton)
  • Experience across tertiary children’s hospitals, regional centres and military medical settings
  • Active involvement in quality-improvement projects and retrospective studies in pediatric GI and general pediatrics

Teaching & Philosophy of Care

  • Regular teaching and supervision of pediatric residents, fellows, and medical students in Canada and the Gulf region
  • Leads case forums, podcast-based teaching groups and exam-oriented sessions for trainees
  • Believes that families deserve clear explanations, honest discussion of options and shared decision-making
  • Strives to minimize pain and anxiety during procedures and to respect each child’s and family’s culture, circumstances and goals
  • Committed to collaborative, evidence-based care that supports growth, development and quality of life for every child

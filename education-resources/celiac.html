Celiac Disease

A family guide to diagnosis, gluten-free basics, and follow-up.

What is celiac disease?

  • An immune reaction to gluten (wheat, barley, rye) that damages the small intestine.

Common symptoms

  • Abdominal pain, diarrhea or constipation, bloating.
  • Poor growth, iron deficiency anemia, fatigue.
  • Some children have minimal GI symptoms.

Diagnosis

  • Blood tests (tTG-IgA and total IgA are common first tests).
  • Sometimes endoscopy/biopsy is needed, depending on results and age.
  • Do not start a gluten-free diet before testing unless your clinician advises—tests can become falsely negative.

Gluten-free basics

  • Avoid: wheat, barley, rye (and foods made with them).
  • Oats are sometimes allowed if labeled gluten-free (discuss with your clinician).
  • Cross-contamination matters (toasters, cutting boards, shared spreads).

Follow-up

  • Repeat blood tests to confirm improvement.
  • Monitor growth and nutrition (iron, vitamin D, etc.).

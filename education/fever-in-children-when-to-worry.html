Fever in children: when to worry

Patient & family education. Updated January 2026. This information is for education only and does not replace individual medical advice.

What this is

Fever is a normal immune response. In most children it is caused by viral infections and improves within a few days.

What matters most

  • Your child’s overall appearance (alert vs very drowsy).
  • Hydration (wet diapers/urination, tears, drinking).
  • Breathing effort.

Home care

  • Offer fluids often; small sips are fine.
  • Dress in light clothing.
  • Fever medicine can improve comfort (use weight-based dosing; avoid aspirin).

Seek urgent care now if

  • Under 3 months with a rectal temperature ≥ 38.0°C.
  • Difficulty breathing, severe lethargy, stiff neck, seizure, or blue lips.
  • Signs of dehydration (very little urine, no tears, very dry mouth).
  • Fever with a new purple/petechial rash.

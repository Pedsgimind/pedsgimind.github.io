Reflux / GERD in Children

Understanding reflux, warning signs, and practical steps that often help.

Normal reflux vs GERD

  • Normal reflux (spit-up) is common in infants and usually improves with time.
  • GERD is reflux that causes complications (poor growth, pain, feeding refusal, breathing symptoms) or significant distress.

What can help

  • Smaller, more frequent feeds (infants).
  • Keep upright after feeds (avoid unsafe sleep positioning; follow safe sleep rules).
  • Avoid tobacco smoke exposure.
  • For older children: avoid late meals; reduce trigger foods if clearly linked.

When to seek medical review

  • Poor weight gain or weight loss.
  • Blood in vomit, black stools, persistent forceful vomiting.
  • Choking, recurrent pneumonia, or significant breathing symptoms.
  • Severe pain or trouble swallowing.
Medication: Acid-suppressing medicines may help some children, but should be guided by a clinician and used when appropriate.

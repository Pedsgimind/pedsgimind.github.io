Common skin rashes in children

Patient & family education. Updated January 2026. This information is for education only and does not replace individual medical advice.

Overview

Most rashes in children are viral or eczema-related and improve with supportive care.

When to seek care

  • Rash with breathing difficulty or facial swelling.
  • Rash with purple spots that do not blanch.
  • Severe pain, blistering, or high fever.

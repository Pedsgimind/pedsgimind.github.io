Acute Diarrhea & Dehydration

Most diarrhea is viral and improves with time—hydration is the priority.

Hydration (most important)

  • Use oral rehydration solution (ORS) if stools are frequent or child is vomiting.
  • Offer small amounts often (teaspoon sips can work).
  • Continue breastfeeding or formula.

What to eat

  • Return to normal foods as tolerated.
  • Avoid sugary drinks (can worsen diarrhea).

Dehydration warning signs

  • Very sleepy, hard to wake, or unusually irritable.
  • Dry mouth, no tears, sunken eyes.
  • Urinating much less than usual.
Seek urgent care if blood in stool, severe belly pain, persistent vomiting, signs of dehydration, or child looks very unwell.
